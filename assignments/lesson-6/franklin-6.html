When It Rains, It Pours

April showers have been extended this year, due to a series of heavy rains storms, which are expected to roll in from the Pacific by nighfall. The total rainfall for the City of Franklin is expected to be between 3-4 inches over the next 36 hours. The citizens of Franklin are busy preparing for the worst, yet still optimistically hoping for the best. Flooding is expected in many of the low lying areas, but the city of Franklin has sent out five city work crews to assist local citizens who have been setting up sand bags. In addition, city workers are warning those in the cities most vulnerable neighborhoods to take precautionary measures. Local churches are also offering their assistance to those in need. More volunteers are needed to help sandbag homes and businesses in the Beaver Creek area before the storm approaches. The mayor has urged city residents to take appropriate safety precautions and to be prepared with adequate food, water, and emergency supplies. To find out how you can help or to learn more about the city's safety guidelines, visit the City of Franklin website.
